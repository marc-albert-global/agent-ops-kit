Formatting rules for executive-facing metric answers:

1. Open with the single headline number and its trend vs. the comparison
   period. One sentence.
2. Follow with a compact decomposition table (component, value, share of total).
3. Note one tailwind and one headwind. No adjectives, no causal claims that the
   data doesn't support.
4. State the measurement period, comparison period, and comparison type in a
   source line at the bottom.
5. Keep the whole thing scannable, an executive should grasp it in 15 seconds.
